Kittur Fort

Kittur is a fort located in the Karnataka state of India, it is the former capital of a minor principality as well as a major archaeological site. Location: JQ2R+FHV, Kittur Fort Rd, Kittur, Karnataka 591115

History

The Kittur Fort was built between 1650 and 1681 by Allappa Gowda Sardesai, the ruler of the Desai dynasty. It later became famous as the stronghold of Rani Chennamma, the brave woman warrior of Karnataka who led a revolt against the British in 1824. Kittur flourished during the reign of Mallasarja Desai and holds historical significance. Within the fort, there is a temple dedicated to Lord Maruti. The area also features other important religious sites, including the Nathapanthi Matha in the police line area and temples dedicated to Kalmeshwara, Dyamavva, and Basavanna. The Basavanna Temple, originally built in the later Chalukyan style, has been fully renovated.
